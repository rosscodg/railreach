About RailReach

RailReach is the United Kingdom's most comprehensive free resource for planning train commutes into London. Designed for homebuyers, renters, relocators, and daily commuters, RailReach provides an interactive colour-coded map displaying journey times from over 350 stations to all 9 London main line terminals: Kings Cross, Euston, Paddington, Waterloo, Victoria, London Bridge, Liverpool Street, Marylebone, and Fenchurch Street. Every journey time is sourced directly from 2026 National Rail timetable data, reflecting the fastest typical weekday services on each route. The platform covers all stations reachable within a 90 minute threshold, spanning commuter belts across the Home Counties, the Midlands, and the South Coast. Whether you need a sub-30-minute commute from Surbiton or are considering a longer journey from Oxford or Brighton, RailReach gives you authoritative, up-to-date data. Stations are colour-coded green, amber, or red so users can instantly assess commute viability. No registration is required. RailReach is the definitive tool for London commute planning.

How accurate is the journey time data?

All journey times on RailReach are sourced from 2026 National Rail timetable data. Times represent the fastest typical weekday service on each route, not off-peak or weekend schedules. Data has been compiled from official operator timetables including Great Western Railway, South Western Railway, Southeastern, Southern, Greater Anglia, LNER, Thameslink, c2c, and Chiltern Railways. Where a station requires a change of train, this is clearly indicated. Times may vary slightly during engineering works or disruption, but represent the standard fastest journey achievable on a normal operating day.

Terminal Summaries

Kings Cross and St Pancras

Kings Cross serves routes northward via Great Northern and Thameslink. Key stations include Kentish Town (4 min), Finsbury Park (5 min), Stevenage (20 min), St Albans City (20 min), Welwyn Garden City (25 min), Hitchin (29 min), Bedford (39 min), and Cambridge (49 min).

Waterloo

Waterloo is the busiest London terminal, serving South Western Railway routes across Surrey, Hampshire, and beyond. Key stations include Vauxhall (4 min), Clapham Junction (7 min), Wimbledon (13 min), Surbiton (17 min), Richmond (18 min), Woking (23 min), Basingstoke (44 min), and Winchester (59 min).

Paddington

Paddington serves Great Western Railway and Elizabeth Line routes westward through the Thames Valley. Key stations include Acton Main Line (7 min), Ealing Broadway (10 min), Slough (15 min), Maidenhead (19 min), Reading (23 min), Didcot Parkway (40 min), Oxford (45 min), and Swindon (50 min).

Victoria

Victoria serves Southeastern and Southern routes into Kent and Sussex. Key stations include Battersea Park (4 min), Clapham Junction (5 min), East Croydon (15 min), Bromley South (18 min), Sevenoaks (30 min), Gatwick Airport (30 min), Tonbridge (40 min), and Brighton (58 min).

London Bridge

London Bridge serves South East London, Kent, and parts of Surrey via Southeastern and Southern. Key stations include New Cross (5 min), Lewisham (8 min), Greenwich-area stations, Dartford (33 min), Redhill (28 min), Rochester (38 min), Sevenoaks (23 min), and Tunbridge Wells (47 min).

Liverpool Street

Liverpool Street serves Greater Anglia routes into Essex, Hertfordshire, and East Anglia. Key stations include Stratford (7 min), Tottenham Hale (12 min), Romford (17 min), Shenfield (24 min), Chelmsford (30 min), Bishops Stortford (37 min), Colchester (45 min), and Ipswich (56 min).

Euston

Euston serves the West Coast Main Line via Avanti and London Northwestern. Key stations include Harrow and Wealdstone (12 min), Watford Junction (16 min), Hemel Hempstead (26 min), Milton Keynes Central (30 min), Leighton Buzzard (32 min), Northampton (49 min), Rugby (52 min), and Coventry (58 min).

Marylebone

Marylebone serves Chiltern Railways routes into Buckinghamshire and the West Midlands. Key stations include Denham (18 min), Gerrards Cross (21 min), Beaconsfield (24 min), High Wycombe (27 min), Princes Risborough (37 min), Great Missenden (38 min), Bicester North (49 min), and Banbury (58 min).

Fenchurch Street

Fenchurch Street serves c2c routes through East London and South Essex. Key stations include Limehouse (4 min), West Ham (6 min), Barking (10 min), Upminster (18 min), Basildon (33 min), Benfleet (42 min), Leigh-on-Sea (47 min), and Southend Central (52 min).
